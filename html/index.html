  1. First Film Streamed

    May 22th, 1993

    Wax or the Discovery of Television Among the Bees, originally released in 1991, is the first film to be streamed on the Internet. Due to bandwidth limitations it is broadcast at 2 frames per second rather than the standard 24 frames per second.

  2. First LiveStream

    September 5th, 1995

    ESPN SportsZone streams a live radio broadcast of a baseball game between the Seattle Mariners and the New York Yankees to thousands of its subscribers worldwide using cutting-edge technology developed by a Seattle-based startup company named Real Networks â€“ the first livestreaming event

  3. MPEG-4 Is Introduced

    November 9th, 1998

    MPEG-4, a method of defining compression of audio and visual (AV) digital data, is introduced

  4. Streaming Feature For Windows Media Player Is Added

    February 30th, 1999

    Microsoft introduces streaming feature in Windows Media Player 6.4. It introduces the ASF file format, which allows storage of multiple video and audio tracks inside a single file. It also introduces Windows Media streaming protocols that support switching streams during broadcast. This technology is most commonly referred to as Multiple Bit Rate ASF, or simply MBR.

  5. Apple Introduced The Video-Containerformat QuickTime

    June 15th, 1999

    Apple introduces a streaming media format in its QuickTime 4 application.

  6. Google Launched Google Video

    January 25th, 2005

    Google Video was a free video hosting service from Google, similar to YouTube, that allowed video clips to be hosted on Google servers and embedded on to other websites. This allowed websites to host lots of video remotely without running into bandwidth or storage-capacity issues

  7. First Youtube Video Is Uploaded

    April 23th, 2005

    YouTube opens for video uploads, and the first YouTube video uploaded on April 23, 2005 is titled Me at the zoo. Between March and July 2006, YouTube grows from 30 to 100 million views of videos per day.

  8. Justin.tv Is Founded

    October 1st, 2006

    Justin.tv, a live-streaming service that is the parent company of Twitch, is founded by Justin Kan.

  9. Amazon Video Is Introduced

    September 7th, 2006

    Amazon introduces video on demand service Amazon Video.

  10. Google Acquires YouTube

    October 9th, 2006

  11. Netflix Announces That It Will Launch Streaming Video

    January 15th, 2007

  12. The Video Element In HTML5 Is Introduced

    February 23th, 2007

    HTML5 specification introduces the video element for the purpose of playing videos. This allows embedding video to no longer necessitate a third-party plugin, as it can be played natively in the browser. HTML5 would later overtake Flash as the primary mechanism for broadcasting video.

  13. Microsoft Silverlight

    September 5th, 2007

    Microsoft introduces Microsoft Silverlight, an application framework for writing and running rich Internet applications, similar to Adobe Flash.

  14. Hulu Launches For Public Acces For Public

    March 12th, 2008

    Hulu, an online streaming service for TV/movies, launches for public access in the United States.

  15. Twitch.tv

    June 6th, 2011

    Justin.tv spins off its gaming division as Twitch.tv, which officially launches in public beta.

  16. Vine Is Created

    June 3rd, 2012

    Vine, a short-form video sharing service where users can share six-second-long looping video clips, is founded by Dom Hofmann, Rus Yusupov, and Colin Kroll.

  17. Instagram Video Sharing

    June 13th, 2013

    Instagram launches video sharing.

  18. FaceBook Live

    January 1st, 2016

    Facebook launches livestreaming to everyone with Facebook Live.
